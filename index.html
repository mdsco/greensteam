How the Thurston Nature Center Started


During the 60's, a conservation movement was sweeping the US and after much discussion the land was turned into the Thurston Nature Center and a pond was built. The purpose of the area was for Ann Arbor Public School's beginning Environmental Educational Program. For many years, all the schools in AA would come to the TNC for field trips. But over time, the pond has become very shallow - decreasing the biodiversity of the plant and animal life. Two summers ago, the pond almost dried up completely, killing all the animals who live only in the water...

Getting the Community Reengaged


...So, a handful of community members are trying to build awareness to restore the pond. Over the years, AAPS teachers have used the pond for various reasons but there has not been one specific curriculum developed with the pond as the focal point. Until now...introducing Green STEAM (science, technology, engineering, art and math). The main purpose of Green STEAM is to increase the biodiversity of the Thurston Nature Center and Pond. The children were given a challenge at the beginning of the year..... they need to prove that the Thurston Nature Center and pond is a good habitat for frogs (the selected animal for the 2014-2015 school year).

Fifth Grade Page

Fifth Graders

Fifth graders are focusing on energy and force by integrating the use of alternative energy to generate power to aerate the oxygen depleted Thurston Nature Center pond. The students discussed the pros and cons of various energy sources. The fifth graders decided on using solar energy to power the aerator and will install the system over the next two months.

Fourth Grade Page

Fourth Graders

Fourth graders focused on the water quality of the Thurston Pond. Students will learn about water conservation, pollution prevention and water filtration. Students conducted water quality tests and designed/built water filters.

Third Grade Page

Third Graders

This year, third graders focused on the habitat of the Thurston Nature Area. Students will learn about habitat requirements for the amphibians in the pond.

Characteristics of amphibians will be studied to determine best habitat locations within our local biomes. In their final project, the students will design their own virtual rain garden.

Second Grade Page

Second Graders

Second graders are focusing on life cycles. Students applied the scientific method to select an amphibian to reintroduce to the pond. Once selected, students design species-specific habitat to meet individual life cycle requirements. Students will observe life cycle from egg through tadpole metamorphosis to mature toad in the classroom nursery habitat. Then, the frogs will be released at the Nature Center.

First Grade Page

First Graders

This year, first graders are focused on collecting different bugs in the Thurston Nature Center to show that the area has food for our frogs. Students collected and recorded the different macro-invertebrates found at Thurston Nature Center Pond. The first graders organized their data in pictographs. Students also discuss how specific macro invertebrates can be indicators of healthy ecosystems.

Kindergarten Page

Kindergarteners

Kindergarteners are focusing on the properties of sand, silt and clay. Students explore the differences in particle size, permeability, and habitat quality. Students designed a habitat model for hibernating frogs. In the spring, they will check the Thurston Pond to verify mud for the frogs to hibernate.
